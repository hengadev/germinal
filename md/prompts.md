# Prompt

## AI Design Builder Prompt — `/upcoming` Page

**Project context**
This website belongs to a high-end event collective focused on curated sensory experiences (sound, taste, space). The brand is minimal, editorial, and premium — black & white, strong typography, restrained use of imagery. No clichés, no flashy UI, no gradients, no playful visuals.

**Page purpose**
Design a page for the route **`/upcoming`** whose sole purpose is to **spotlight the next upcoming event**.
This is **not** an events list and **not** a classic event detail page.
It should feel like an editorial reveal and create anticipation.

The page must answer:

* Why this event exists *now*
* What makes it special compared to previous ones
* What is already known vs what is still intentionally unrevealed

---

### Overall tone & layout

* Calm, confident, editorial
* Strong hierarchy, generous white space
* Vertical rhythm similar to a magazine feature
* Desktop-first but responsive
* Black & white only (subtle grayscale allowed)
* No cards, no shadows, no decorative UI elements

---

### Page structure (mandatory)

#### 1. Intro / Statement (above the fold)

* Large, typographic headline
* Short, poetic statement describing the intention of the upcoming event
* Avoid dates or logistics here
* Example tone: curatorial, intentional, restrained

CTA (secondary, discreet):

* “Register interest”
* or “Be notified”

---

#### 2. Event Identity (partial reveal)

* Event name (strong typographic treatment)
* Location (city only, no address)
* Date or time window (exact or approximate)
* Optional subtle divider or typographic separator

This section should feel **deliberate, not promotional**.

---

#### 3. Curatorial Focus

A text-led section explaining:

* What is being explored in this edition
* The atmosphere, themes, or sensorial direction
* How it differs from previous events

Layout:

* One main column of text
* Optional pull quote or emphasized sentence

---

#### 4. What’s Confirmed / What’s Unfolding

Two contrasting sections or columns:

**Confirmed**

* Core elements that are locked (format, duration, general experience type)

**Unfolding**

* Elements intentionally not revealed yet (artists, menu, space details)

This reinforces anticipation and restraint.

---

#### 5. Visual Mood (minimal)

* One or two large images OR abstract placeholders
* Images should feel atmospheric, not descriptive
* Could be blurred, cropped, or monochrome
* No galleries, no carousels

---

#### 6. Call to Engagement

End the page with a clear but minimal CTA:

* Email signup
* Early access notification
* “Follow the project”

This is **not** a ticket purchase CTA.

---

### Things to explicitly avoid

* Event cards
* Calendars
* Timelines
* Ticket pricing tables
* Over-designed buttons
* Loud colors or decorative patterns
* Anything resembling a booking or listing page

---

### UX intent

The user should leave the page feeling:

* Curious
* Informed, but not saturated
* Emotionally connected to what’s coming
* Aware that this event is *singular and intentional*
