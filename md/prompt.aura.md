## **UI Design Context Prompt – Germinal Landing Page**

You are designing the **public landing page** for a web application called **Germinal**.

---

## 1. What the application is

Germinal is a **curated cultural platform** that presents:

* Selected **events**
* The **talents** who work with and shape those events

It is **editorial**, **intentional**, and **restrained**.

This is not:

* A social network
* A marketplace
* A startup marketing page

Think:

* Exhibition catalogue
* Cultural program
* Contemporary art institution website

---

## 2. Global tone & design language (critical)

**Mood**

* Minimal
* Calm
* High-end
* Timeless
* Confident but quiet

**Color**

* Strictly black, white, and grey
* No accent colors
* No gradients
* High contrast used sparingly

**Typography**

* Clear hierarchy
* Elegant, readable type
* Titles feel editorial, not promotional
* Generous spacing between elements

**Layout**

* Strong use of negative space
* Sections clearly separated
* No visual clutter
* Everything feels deliberate

---

## 3. Navigation (exact specification)

The navigation is **simple and fixed at the top**.

It contains **only three links**:

* **Events**
* **Talents**
* **Contact**

Navigation behavior:

* Clean horizontal layout
* No icons
* No background color by default
* Subtle hover states only (underline or opacity change)
* Should feel light and almost invisible

---

## 4. Hero Section (above the fold)

### Visual

* Full-width hero section
* Background image:
  **A table set for an event** (editorial photography, natural light, elegant, not staged like marketing)
* A **dark overlay** on top of the image (black with opacity)
* Overlay should soften the image, not fully hide it

### Text on top

* Centered or left-aligned (either is acceptable, but keep it calm)
* Short, restrained copy
* Avoid slogans, buzzwords, or calls to action

Examples of acceptable tone (do not copy literally):

* “A curated selection of events and the people behind them”
* “Events, talents, and moments brought together”

Typography:

* Clear contrast against the overlay
* Title + short supporting line only
* No buttons in the hero

---

## 5. Events Section

Purpose:

* Introduce a **selection of upcoming or highlighted events**

Content:

* Section title (simple, e.g. “Events”)
* A small number of **Event cards** (reuse the event card design language)
* Cards should feel curated, not like a feed

Call to action:

* A simple text link or minimal button:

  * “View all events”
* No icons
* No aggressive styling

Layout:

* Calm grid or horizontal row
* Plenty of breathing space

---

## 6. Talents Section

Purpose:

* Introduce the **people** behind the events

Content:

* Section title (e.g. “Talents”)
* A small selection of **Talent cards** or previews
* Visual consistency with Events section

Call to action:

* Minimal text link or button:

  * “View all talents”

Layout:

* Mirrors the Events section for balance
* Feels intentional and structured

---

## 7. What NOT to include

* No testimonials
* No metrics or numbers
* No marketing copy
* No animations beyond subtle hover effects
* No background patterns or decorative elements

---

## 8. Design principle

> “The page should feel like an invitation, not an advertisement.”

Everything should feel composed, restrained, and high-value.
